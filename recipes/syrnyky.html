Syrnyky

Description

Syrnyky are soft, fluffy cheese pancakes made from cottage cheese or farmer’s cheese. They're a popular breakfast or dessert in Ukraine and are often served with sour cream, honey, or jam. Syrnyky are light and slightly sweet, with a rich, creamy interior and a crispy golden crust from frying. They're incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed warm, straight from the pan.

Ingredients

  • 500g (about 2 cups) farmer's cheese or cottage cheese (drained)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp sugar (optional, depending on how sweet you want them)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder (optional, for extra fluffiness)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or butter (for frying)
  • Sour cream, honey, or jam (for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Cheese Mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine the farmer's cheese (or cottage cheese), egg, sugar, vanilla extract, salt, and flour. Mix everything until smooth. You may need to mash the cheese a bit if it's too lumpy, and add a little more flour if the dough is too wet.
  2. Form the Syrnyky: Dust your hands and a clean surface with a bit of flour. Take a small amount of the cheese mixture (about 2-3 tbsp) and form it into a small patty, about 2-3 inches in diameter. Repeat with the remaining mixture, creating little rounds.
  3. Heat the Pan: In a large pan, heat the oil or butter over medium heat. Once it's hot, carefully place the syrnyky into the pan, leaving space between each one.
  4. Fry Until Golden: Fry the syrnyky for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. Flip them carefully using a spatula, so they don’t break.
  5. Serve: Remove the syrnyky from the pan and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve warm with a dollop of sour cream, a drizzle of honey, or your favorite jam.
